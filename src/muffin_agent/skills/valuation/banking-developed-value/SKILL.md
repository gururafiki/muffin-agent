# Banking — Developed Markets Value Strategy

**Primary Valuation Method:** Dual-metric approach with **P/B Ratio + P/E Ratio**

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Target Range | Assessment |
|--------|--------------|------------|
| **Price-to-Book (P/B)** | 0.8-2.0x | Below 1.0 suggests distressed asset quality; >2.0 implies strong profitability or market trust |
| **Price-to-Earnings (P/E)** | 10-15x | Varies with ROE; lower P/E if ROE depressed by rate environment |
| **Return on Equity (ROE)** | >12% | Quality threshold; banks below 10% may be struggling |
| **Net Interest Margin (NIM)** | 2.0-3.5% | Critical income driver; track margin trends |
| **Non-Performing Loan Ratio** | <2% | Higher ratios signal credit quality deterioration |
| **Cost-to-Income Ratio** | <55% | Efficiency metric; lower is better (50% excellent) |
| **Capital Ratio (Tier 1)** | >12% | Regulatory requirement, buffer for stress |

## Critical Screening Questions

1. Is P/B discount justified by ROE weakness or genuine opportunity?
2. Can the bank sustain dividend at current payout ratio during stress scenarios?
3. What's the trajectory of NIM (up or down with rate environment)?
4. How much of earnings growth is organic vs accounting benefits from rate increases?
