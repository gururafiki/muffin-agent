# Emerging Markets Valuation Adjustments

## General EM Discount

- **Valuation discount 30-40% vs developed markets** typical
- Discount driven by company-specific factors, not a single country risk premium
- Currency volatility: add 10-15% discount for emerging market FX risk

## Country-Specific Considerations

**China:**
- Trade at 40% discount to US equities on 1-year forward P/E basis (2026)
- Geopolitical tensions, capital controls, regulatory unpredictability
- Property sector linkages affect many sectors

**India:**
- Currently trading at 66% premium to MSCI EM (narrowed from historical 110%)
- Strong growth story but valuations already reflect optimism
- INR depreciation ~5% annually typical

**Poland:**
- 60-70% labor cost advantage vs Western Europe influences valuation
- EU member: regulatory certainty, market access
- Lower country premium than non-EU emerging markets (10-20% premium to non-EU CEE)

**Brazil:**
- Structural risks: government interference, political uncertainty
- BRL volatile (10-15% moves common)
- Cost advantages in natural resources and agriculture

**Thailand:**
- Developed EM market with moderate risk profile
- Tourism and manufacturing exposure
