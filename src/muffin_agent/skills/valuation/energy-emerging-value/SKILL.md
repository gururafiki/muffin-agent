# Energy — Emerging Markets Value Strategy

**Method:** FCF Yield + Sovereign Risk Assessment + Currency Risk

## Key Metrics & Adjustments

| Metric | DM Target | EM Target | Risk Factor |
|--------|-----------|-----------|------------|
| **FCF Yield** | 6-10% | 8-15% | Higher risk = higher required return |
| **Dividend Yield** | 4-8% | 6-12% | Political risk premium |
| **Cost per Barrel (TAC)** | Competitive | Advantaged | Brazil/India often lower cost |
| **Debt/EBITDA** | <2.0x | <2.5x | Higher leverage acceptable |
| **Currency Risk** | - | -5-10% additional adjustment | FX conversion/control risk |

## Brazil Oil (Petrobras)

- FCF yield 27.8% (2025) exceptionally high
- Structural risks: government interference, dividend policy, capital allocation
- Cost advantage: offshore fields competitive vs global
- Real currency depreciation: makes costs lower in USD terms

## India Oil (ONGC)

- Controlled by Indian government (policy uncertainty)
- Domestic market focus (60%+ revenue)
- Reserve replacement challenges
- Growth potential: limited (mature fields, exploration struggles)
