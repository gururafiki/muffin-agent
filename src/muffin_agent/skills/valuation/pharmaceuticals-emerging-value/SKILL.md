# Pharmaceuticals — Emerging Markets Value Strategy

**Method:** P/E with Regional Business Model Considerations

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Target | Regional Adjustment |
|--------|--------|---------------------|
| **P/E Ratio** | 8-12x | Lower than DM; biosimilar/generic pressure |
| **Gross Margin** | 50-65% | Manufacturing cost advantage (India) |
| **R&D as % Revenue** | 10-15% | Lower R&D intensity than DM multinationals |
| **Export Revenue Mix** | 40%+ | Critical for valuation (export-dependent) |
| **Biosimilar Penetration** | Growing | Market opportunity but margin compression |
| **Patent Cliff Risk** | Low | Most products off-patent; recurring revenue model |

## India Pharma

- Generics and biosimilars dominate; limited branded portfolio
- Valuation depends on: export markets (40%+ US/EU), domestic formulations, API manufacturing
- Growth drivers: biosimilar uptake (10-15% of market now vs 3% 2015), export expansion

## China Pharma

- Domestic focus (60% revenue from China typically)
- Innovation focus increasing (policy support)
- Bifurcated: state-owned (stable, lower margins) vs private (growth, higher risk)
