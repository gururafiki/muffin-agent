## DOCUMENT OVERVIEW

This guide provides specific valuation methodologies for different stock combinations:
- **9 Major Sectors** with key sub-categories
- **Market Types:** Developed Markets vs Emerging Markets
- **Stock Types:** Value vs Growth
- **Geographic Coverage:** US, UK, Germany, France, Netherlands, Japan (Developed); China, India, Poland, Thailand, Brazil (Emerging)

**Critical Principle:** Valuation methodology selection depends on the company's fundamental characteristics. A stock classified as "value" in one sector may use entirely different metrics than a "value" stock in another sector.

## SECTION-WIDE GUIDELINES

### Metrics Applicable to Multiple Stock Types (Avoid Repetition)

**Consumer Discretionary Sector:**
- Use **P/S (Price-to-Sales)** instead of P/E where earnings are volatile or negative
- P/E can be misleading during cyclical downturns; P/S provides stability
- EV/EBITDA 8-12x typical for value, higher for growth

**Growth Stocks Across All Sectors:**
- **Operating margin is NOT the primary metric**
- Focus instead on revenue growth, market share, and customer metrics
- Profitability threshold: Break-even to positive FCF acceptable if growth justifies it
- **PEG Ratio < 2.0** generally indicates attractive entry for growth stocks

**Value Stocks Across All Sectors:**
- **Operating margin quality critical** (8-12% minimum depending on sector)
- Free cash flow yield > 6% typical entry point
- ROIC > 10% benchmark for capital allocation quality

**Pharmaceutical/Drug Portfolio Analysis (Healthcare Sector):**
- Analyze major drugs individually, not as aggregate
- Patent expiration timeline: map revenue cliff by drug
- **Patent cliffs cause 80-90% revenue erosion** in 2-3 years after expiration
- Example: Pfizer's Lipitor lost $10B revenue after patent expiration

**Emerging Markets Adjustments:**
- **Valuation discount 30-40% vs developed markets** typical (not from single country risk premium, but company-specific factors)
- Poland: 60-70% labor cost advantage vs Western Europe influences valuation
- Currency volatility: Add 10-15% discount for emerging market FX risk
- China: Trade at 40% discount to US equities on 1-year forward P/E basis (2026)
- India: Currently trading at 66% premium to MSCI EM (narrowed from historical 110%)

## SECTOR 1: FINANCIAL SERVICES - BANKING

### Developed Markets (US, UK, Germany, France)

#### VALUE STRATEGY

**Primary Valuation Method:** Dual-metric approach with **P/B Ratio + P/E Ratio**

**Key Metrics:**

| Metric | Target Range | Assessment |
|--------|--------------|------------|
| **Price-to-Book (P/B)** | 0.8-2.0x | Below 1.0 suggests distressed asset quality; >2.0 implies strong profitability or market trust |
| **Price-to-Earnings (P/E)** | 10-15x | Varies with ROE; lower P/E if ROE depressed by rate environment |
| **Return on Equity (ROE)** | >12% | Quality threshold; banks below 10% may be struggling |
| **Net Interest Margin (NIM)** | 2.0-3.5% | Critical income driver; track margin trends |
| **Non-Performing Loan Ratio** | <2% | Higher ratios signal credit quality deterioration |
| **Cost-to-Income Ratio** | <55% | Efficiency metric; lower is better (50% excellent) |
| **Capital Ratio (Tier 1)** | >12% | Regulatory requirement, buffer for stress |

**Critical Screening Questions:**
1. Is P/B discount justified by ROE weakness or genuine opportunity?
2. Can the bank sustain dividend at current payout ratio during stress scenarios?
3. What's the trajectory of NIM (up or down with rate environment)?
4. How much of earnings growth is organic vs accounting benefits from rate increases?

**Why Dual Metrics:**
- **P/B alone** can mislead during rapid earnings changes
- **P/E alone** ignores balance sheet quality (critical for banks)
- **P/B + P/E cross-check** provides complete picture

#### GROWTH STRATEGY

**Primary Method:** P/E with **ROE expansion thesis**

**Key Metrics:**

| Metric | Target Range | Strategic Significance |
|--------|--------------|------------------------|
| **P/E Ratio** | 12-20x | Higher acceptable if ROE expanding past 15% |
| **Return on Equity** | >15% | Growth thesis requires superior profitability |
| **Loan Growth Rate** | 12%+ YoY | Market share gains; outpacing deposit base growth |
| **Net Interest Margin** | Expanding | Rising rate environment boosts NIM; assess durability |
| **Cost-to-Income Ratio** | Declining | Digital transformation, automation opportunities |
| **Return on Assets (ROA)** | >1.0% | Operating leverage improvement |

**Growth Validation:**
- Ensure loan growth sustainable (supported by capital, deposits)
- Rising NIM should not depend entirely on rate environment (check if structural improvement)
- Cost-to-income improvement from real operational efficiency, not one-time items

### Emerging Markets (China, India, Poland)

#### VALUE STRATEGY

**Primary Method:** P/B + Country Risk Adjustment + Asset Quality Deep Dive

**Key Metrics & Adjustments:**

| Metric | Target Range | EM Adjustment |
|--------|--------------|----------------|
| **P/B Ratio** | 0.5-1.5x | 30-40% deeper discount vs developed (China: 0.6-1.0x typical) |
| **P/E Ratio** | 8-12x | Reflects higher business risk |
| **Required ROE** | >15% | Threshold higher than developed markets |
| **Currency Risk Premium** | +2-3% | Added to discount rate for analysis |
| **Political/Regulatory Risk** | Case-by-case | China: geopolitical; India: regulatory clarity; Poland: EU stability |

**Country-Specific Considerations:**

**China Banks:**
- Valuation discount 40% vs US banks justified by: geopolitical tensions, property sector links, capital controls
- Asset quality: scrutinize non-standard loans and "evergreen" credit practices
- Growth: slower now (5-8% loan growth) vs historical 15%+
- P/B 0.6-0.9x typical for major state-owned banks

**India Banks:**
- Valuation premium 66% to MSCI EM (2026), but narrowed from 110% historically
- Loan growth 10-12% YoY attractive
- Asset quality: monitor agricultural loan stress, commercial real estate
- P/B 0.9-1.5x reasonable range

**Poland Banks:**
- Lower risk than other EM (EU member, stable currency PLN)
- Valuation closer to developed markets (P/B 1.0-1.5x)
- Earning growth 8-12% from market consolidation
- Lower country premium than non-EU emerging markets

#### GROWTH STRATEGY

**Method:** ROE Expansion + Market Penetration + Credit Growth

**Metrics:**

| Metric | Target | Validation |
|--------|--------|------------|
| **Loan Portfolio Growth** | 15-25% YoY | Penetration of underbankified populations |
| **Credit Growth vs GDP** | 2-3x GDP growth | Sustainability check |
| **Return on Assets (ROA)** | >1.2% | Efficiency in growth phase |
| **Deposit Base Stability** | Growing 10%+ YoY | Deposit franchise strength |
| **Cost-to-Income** | <60% | Acceptable during growth phase |

**Risk Validation:**
- Ensure credit growth not driven by easing standards (check portfolio mix)
- Deposit growth supports lending growth (avoid reliance on expensive wholesale funding)
- Capital ratios can support growth (Tier 1 >12%)

## SECTOR 2: FINANCIAL SERVICES - INSURANCE

### General Insurance (Developed Markets)

#### VALUE STRATEGY

**Primary Method:** **Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio + Combined Ratio + Investment Income**

**Key Metrics:**

| Metric | Target | Assessment |
|--------|--------|------------|
| **P/B Ratio** | 0.7-1.5x | Fair value typically 1.0-1.2x; below 0.9 signals value opportunity |
| **Combined Ratio** | <100% | <100% = underwriting profit; >100% = underwriting loss |
| **Underwriting Profit Margin** | >5% | Combined Ratio converts to margin: 95% CR = 5% margin |
| **Investment Yield** | 4-5%+ | UK gilt yields 4.45% (Feb 2026); use as benchmark |
| **Dividend Yield** | 3-5% | Sustainability check vs combined ratio |
| **Expense Ratio** | <30% | Operating efficiency as % of premium |

**Benchmark Companies:**
- UK Motor Insurance: Admiral Group maintained combined ratio <90% (exceptional; industry average 95-110%)
- General insurers trade 0.7-1.0x P/B during market peaks, 1.0-1.5x during troughs

**Investment Income Impact:**
- Rising gilt yields (2026: 4.45%) boost insurer profitability significantly vs 2016-2021 near-zero environment
- Investment income now 20-30% of total earnings for well-positioned insurers
- Reinvestment risk if rates fall; assess portfolio duration

#### GROWTH STRATEGY

**Method:** Combined Ratio Improvement Trajectory + Market Share + Pricing Power

**Metrics:**

| Metric | Target | Drivers |
|--------|--------|---------|
| **Combined Ratio Trend** | Moving toward 90% | Underwriting discipline, expense control |
| **Premium Growth** | 7-10% YoY | Market share gains or pricing increases |
| **Expense Ratio Decline** | -100 to -200 bps | Scale, automation, distribution efficiency |
| **Investment Yield Stability** | Maintained | Avoid portfolio duration extension as rates may fall |
| **Retention Rate** | >85% | Customer loyalty; higher is better |

### Life Insurance (Developed Markets)

#### VALUE STRATEGY

**Primary Method:** **Embedded Value (EV) Multiple + New Business Value**

**Key Metrics:**

| Metric | Formula/Target | Interpretation |
|--------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Embedded Value (EV)** | PVFP + ANAV | PVFP = Present Value Future Profits; ANAV = Adjusted Net Asset Value |
| **EV Multiple** | Price/EVPS (Earnings Per Share equivalent) | 0.6-1.0x typical for mature insurers (conservative) |
| **PVFP (Value in Force)** | NPV of profits from current policies | Declining as in-force book runs off |
| **Adjusted Net Asset Value** | Capital + Surplus adjusted to market | Balance sheet valuation |
| **New Business Contribution** | Annual profit from new policies written | Sustainability of EV growth |

**Why Embedded Value for Life Insurance:**
- EPS/P/E ratio highly distorted by: accounting depreciation, one-time gains, policy surrenders
- MCEV (Market Consistent Embedded Value) provides clearer picture of shareholder value creation
- Common in Europe and Asia; less used in North America

**Valuation Process:**
1. Calculate EV (PVFP + ANAV)
2. Compare P/EV multiple to peers and historical average
3. Assess sustainability: Is EV growing or declining?
4. Examine new business profitability (profitable growth = multiple expansion potential)

## SECTOR 3: TECHNOLOGY - SOFTWARE/SaaS

### Developed Markets (US, UK, Germany)

#### VALUE STRATEGY

**Primary Method:** **PEG Ratio + EV/Revenue + Net Revenue Retention (NRR)**

**Key Metrics:**

| Metric | Target | Significance |
|--------|--------|--------------|
| **PEG Ratio** | <1.0 | Stock fairly valued if PEG = 1; <1.0 undervalued |
| **EV/Revenue** | 5-8x | Lower end of SaaS range; growth at 8-15x |
| **Revenue Growth Rate** | 20-30% YoY | Minimum for SaaS to justify premium valuation |
| **Net Revenue Retention (NRR)** | >100% | Existing customers expand usage; expansion revenue key |
| **Gross Margin** | >60% | SaaS standard; software leverage |
| **Rule of 40** | Growth + FCF Margin >40 | Balanced value: 20% growth + 20% FCF margin = healthy |
| **Free Cash Flow Margin** | >15% | Path to profitability |

**NRR Deep Dive:**
- **NRR = (Beginning ARR + Expansion - Churn) / Beginning ARR**
- >100% = expanding from existing customers (very valuable)
- 120%+ NRR commands premium multiples; 10-pt NRR increase = 20-30% valuation uplift
- **Gross Retention Rate (GRR)** increasingly important; 1.5-2.0x ARR multiple premium for top-quartile GRR

**PEG Calculation Example:**
- Company P/E = 25x, expected growth = 25% â†’ PEG = 25/25 = 1.0 (fairly valued)
- If same company trades 22x with 25% growth â†’ PEG = 0.88 (undervalued)

#### GROWTH STRATEGY

**Method:** PEG 1.0-2.5 + Revenue Growth Acceleration + Customer Expansion Metrics

**Metrics:**

| Metric | Target | Validation |
|--------|--------|------------|
| **Revenue Growth** | 25%+ YoY | Minimum for premium valuation |
| **PEG Ratio** | 1.5-2.0 | Acceptable if NRR >120% and strong unit economics |
| **EV/Revenue** | 10-15x | Premium multiples justified by growth |
| **NRR** | >120% | Land-and-expand model working |
| **Gross Retention Rate** | >90% | Sticky product; low churn |
| **Rule of 40 Target** | 30% growth + 10% FCF margin | Balanced profitability |
| **Magic Number** | >0.75 | (Current quarter ARR growth rate / prior quarter S&M spend) indicates CAC efficiency |
| **CAC Payback Period** | <18 months | Customer acquisition cost paid back quickly |

**Growth Validation:**
- Ensure revenue growth not slowing (watch quarterly trend)
- NRR stability check: Is expansion revenue decelerating?
- Unit economics improving or worsening (CAC payback critical)

## SECTOR 4: HEALTHCARE - PHARMACEUTICALS

### Developed Markets (US, UK, Germany, France)

#### VALUE STRATEGY

**Primary Method:** **P/E Ratio + Risk-Adjusted NPV (rNPV) of Pipeline**

**Key Metrics:**

| Metric | Target | Assessment |
|--------|--------|------------|
| **P/E Ratio** | 10-15x | Currently at multi-decade lows (Sanofi 12x, Feb 2026) |
| **Operating Margin** | 25-35% | Large pharma typical; smaller biotech 0% acceptable |
| **R&D Efficiency** | 15-25% of revenue | Benchmark; above 25% may indicate pipeline drought |
| **Dividend Yield** | 2-4% | Mature pharmas pay dividends from stable core |
| **Patent Cliff Timeline** | Next 5 years | Critical: map revenue loss by drug |
| **Earnings Quality** | Core vs reported | Adjust for acquisitions, one-time writedowns |

**Patent Cliff Analysis (CRITICAL):**

**Method:**
1. Identify top 5-10 drugs by revenue contribution
2. Map patent expiration dates for each
3. Model 80-90% revenue loss in 2-3 years after patent expiration
4. Assess pipeline drugs to replace lost revenue

**Example - Patent Cliff Risk:**
- Blockbuster drug: $3B annual revenue, patent expires Year 3
- Expected Year 4 revenue: $300-600M (83-85% loss)
- Company must have pipeline drugs at advanced stage to offset
- If pipeline insufficient, earnings face cliff; P/E should reflect risk

**Pipeline Probability of Success (PoS):**
- Phase 1 trials: ~30% cumulative PoS to regulatory approval
- Phase 2: ~30% cumulative PoS
- Phase 3: ~60% cumulative PoS
- Regulatory: ~90% for NDA/MAA submission
- Use these to risk-weight pipeline value

**Risk-Adjusted NPV (rNPV) Calculation:**
```
rNPV = Î£ [Commercial CF_t Ã— Cumulative P(success to year t)] / (1+r)^t
```
- CF = expected commercial cash flows
- P = probability-weighted by phase
- Discount rate 8-12% typical (lower than standard WACC; risk in probabilities)

#### GROWTH STRATEGY

**Method:** rNPV of Pipeline + New Product Launches + Market Expansion

**Metrics:**

| Metric | Target | Strategic Implication |
|--------|--------|----------------------|
| **P/E Ratio** | 15-25x | Premium if strong Phase 3 data |
| **Blockbuster Pipeline** | 2-3 drugs Phase 3 | $500M+ peak sales forecast |
| **Clinical Trial Success** | Phase 3 efficacy data | Market impact, peak sales confirmation |
| **Patent Cliff Timeline** | No material cliff next 5 years | Or strong pipeline replacement |
| **Gross Margin Trend** | Stable/expanding | Cost of goods control important |

**New Drug Peak Sales Forecasting:**
- Market size Ã— expected penetration rate
- Example: $50B pain market Ã— 10% share = $5B potential
- But discount for: side effects, competitive entrants, pricing pressure
- Realistic peak: $2-3B for successful differentiated drug

## SECTOR 5: REAL ESTATE - REITs

### Developed Markets (US, UK, Germany, France)

#### VALUE STRATEGY

**Primary Method:** **Price-to-NAV (P/NAV) + FFO & AFFO Multiples + Cap Rate Analysis**

**Key Metrics:**

| Metric | Formula/Target | Significance |
|--------|-----------------|--------------|
| **Net Asset Value (NAV)** | Fair Value of Properties - Liabilities | Base case valuation |
| **Capitalization Rate (Cap Rate)** | Property NOI / Market Value | Prime urban: 3-5%; suburban: 6-9% |
| **P/FFO Multiple** | Price per Share / FFO per Share | Primary REIT valuation multiple (12-18x) |
| **P/AFFO Multiple** | Price per Share / AFFO per Share | Closer to true distributable cash (14-20x) |
| **FFO Calculation** | Net Income + Depreciation - Gain on Sales | Recurring operating earnings |
| **AFFO Calculation** | FFO - Maintenance CapEx | Sustainable distribution |
| **Dividend Yield** | Annual Distribution / Share Price | 3-5% typical |
| **Premium/Discount to NAV** | (Price - NAV) / NAV | Trading at premium = market confidence |
| **Debt/EBITDA** | Total Debt / EBITDA | <5.0x healthy for REITs |

**Cap Rate Analysis:**
- Prime office (CBD): 3.5-4.5% (lowest risk/return)
- Suburban office: 5-6%
- Retail (prime): 4-5%
- Industrial/logistics: 3.5-4.5% (high demand)
- Residential: 3-4.5% (stable cash flow)
- **Lower cap rate = higher property valuation**
- As cap rates compress (from 7.7% Q3 2025 to current), property values rise; REITs benefit

**Example NAV Calculation:**
- Property 1: NOI $5M / 5% cap rate = $100M value
- Property 2: NOI $3M / 6% cap rate = $50M value
- Total properties: $150M
- Less: Total liabilities ($50M)
- **NAV = $100M**

#### GROWTH STRATEGY

**Method:** FFO/AFFO Growth + NOI Growth + Cap Rate Compression

**Metrics:**

| Metric | Target | Driver |
|--------|--------|--------|
| **FFO Growth** | 5-10% annually | Organic NOI growth + acquisitions |
| **NOI Growth** | Positive 3-5%+ | Rent growth + occupancy gains + operational efficiency |
| **Occupancy Rate** | >90% | Leasing momentum; portfolio quality |
| **Tenant Credit Quality** | Grade A dominant | Payment reliability |
| **Cap Rate Compression** | 25-50 bps annually | Property value appreciation |
| **Dividend Growth** | 2-4% annually | Supported by FFO growth |
| **AFFO Payout Ratio** | 80-90% | Sustainable distribution + retention |

## SECTOR 7: CONSUMER DISCRETIONARY

**Note:** This sector uses **P/S (Price-to-Sales) more than P/E** due to earnings volatility during cycles.

### Developed Markets (US, UK, Germany)

#### VALUE STRATEGY

**Primary Method:** **P/S Ratio + EV/EBITDA + ROIC Analysis**

**Why P/S Over P/E:**
- Earnings highly cyclical; P/E can be misleading
- During downturns, earnings approach zero or go negative
- P/S more stable metric; reflects business scale
- EV/EBITDA provides capital structure adjustment

**Key Metrics:**

## APPLYING THE GUIDE: PRACTICAL EXAMPLES

### Example 1: Financial Services (Banking) - Developed Market Value Stock

**Scenario:** UK bank trading at 0.9x P/B, P/E 11x

**Valuation Analysis:**
1. **P/B 0.9x Assessment:** Below 1.0 signals either distressed assets OR genuine opportunity
   - Check: NPL ratio (<2% healthy), capital ratio (>12% buffer)
   - If healthy, P/B <1.0 = undervaluation

2. **P/E 11x Assessment:** Below peer average 12-14x
   - Compare ROE to peers: Is this bank earning lower ROE (justifying lower P/E)?
   - Or earning similar ROE at cheaper multiple (value opportunity)?

3. **Combined Assessment:**
   - If ROE 11%, P/E 11x = "fair" by Peter Lynch rule (PEG-like analysis)
   - If ROE 13%, P/E 11x = undervalued (PEG <1.0)

4. **Decision:** Buy if:
   - Asset quality confirmed (NPL <2%)
   - Capital ratios strong
   - NIM stable or expanding
   - Dividend sustainable

### Example 2: Technology (SaaS) - Emerging Market Growth Stock

**Scenario:** India SaaS company, revenue $20M, growing 50% YoY, NRR 110%

**Valuation Analysis:**

1. **PEG Ratio Check:**
   - Trading at 15x revenue (EV/Revenue)
   - Growth 50% YoY
   - Comparable: US SaaS at 25% growth trading 10x = PEG 0.4x
   - India SaaS at 50% growth trading 15x = PEG 0.3x
   - **Assessment: Cheap on growth basis, but need unit economics check**

2. **Unit Economics Deep Dive:**
   - CAC Payback Period: How long to recover customer acquisition cost?
   - Target: <18 months acceptable
   - GRR (Gross Retention): >85% minimum for emerging market SaaS
   - NRR 110% is good but build curve may be volatile

3. **Risk Factors:**
   - Currency risk: INR depreciation 5-10% annually typical
   - Customer credit risk: Emerging market customers more likely to default/churn
   - Payment infrastructure: Credit card adoption, payment processing costs higher

4. **Decision:** Reasonable valuation IF:
   - CAC payback <18 months demonstrated
   - GRR >85% confirmed for 2+ years
   - Customer concentration managed (top 10 <30% revenue)
   - Currency hedging or natural hedge in place

### Example 3: Healthcare (Pharmaceutical) - Developed Market Value Stock

**Scenario:** Large pharma, P/E 12x, blockbuster drug (40% revenue) expires in Year 3

**Valuation Analysis:**

1. **Patent Cliff Quantification:**
   - Blockbuster current revenue: $4B annually
   - Year 3 patent expiration expected
   - Post-expiration revenue: $400-800M (80-90% loss)
   - This represents $3-3.6B annual revenue loss in Year 4
   - **Must find replacement in pipeline or face earnings cliff**

2. **Pipeline Replacement Assessment:**
   - Phase 3 drugs in development: $2B+ peak sales potential?
   - Phase 2 drugs: Need 5-6 year timeline, high risk
   - **Gap: Is replacement pipeline adequate?**

3. **Risk-Adjusted Valuation:**
   - Current earnings: $1.0 EPS (20% operating margin on $5B revenue)
   - Year 3 cliff: Reduce by $3B revenue = $0.4 EPS equivalent (assuming margin stable)
   - Add back pipeline rNPV: If pipeline = $1.5B PV of future cash flows = $0.3 EPS equivalent
   - **Normalized earnings power: $0.4 + $0.3 = $0.7 EPS**
   - Fair value: $0.7 EPS Ã— 15x multiple = $10.50/share
   - Current price at P/E 12x on $1.0 EPS = $12/share
   - **Conclusion: Fairly valued or slight discount acceptable IF pipeline credible**

### Example 5: Consumer Staples - Emerging Market Value Stock

**Scenario:** India FMCG company, P/E 14x, revenue growth 8% (3% volume, 5% pricing)

**Valuation Analysis:**

1. **Volume vs Pricing Breakdown (Critical):**
   - Total growth 8%: 3% volume + 5% pricing
   - **Question: Is 5% annual pricing sustainable?**
   - Consumer staples have pricing power, but not unlimited
   - India 2021-2024: Inflation ~6% average; pricing 5-7% absorbed
   - Going forward: If inflation moderates to 4%, pricing should follow

2. **Margin Sustainability:**
   - Gross margin stable? (key question)
   - Or margin pressure from cost inflation not fully passed through?
   - If volume growing only 3%, company needs pricing for total growth
   - Risk: If pricing stops, growth drops to 3% (volume-only)

3. **Valuation Check:**
   - P/E 14x on 8% growth
   - PEG = 14/8 = 1.75 (reasonable but not cheap)
   - Dividend yield 2.5%: Total return = 2.5% + 5% growth = 7.5% acceptable?
   - For emerging market, this is mid-range return (6-10% typical)

4. **Decision:** Fair value IF:
   - Volume growth 3%+ sustainable (category expansion)
   - Pricing power confirmed (brand strength)
   - Operating margin maintained despite input cost inflation
   - Dividend sustainable (payout <50% earnings)

## CRITICAL FINAL REMINDERS

1. **Discount Rates in Emerging Markets:**
   - McKinsey research: Do NOT simply add 3-5% country risk premium
   - Instead: Stress test specific business risks in scenario analysis
   - Triangulate: DCF + comparable multiples + scenario analysis

2. **Valuation Errors to Avoid:**
   - Banking: Don't use P/E alone (balance sheet matters)
   - SaaS: Don't ignore unit economics (NRR impressive but CAC high = burn)
   - Pharma: Don't ignore patent cliffs (single-drug risk)
   - REITs: Don't ignore cap rate environment (falling rates = rising valuations)
   - Energy: Don't use P/E (earnings too volatile; use FCF)

3. **Currency Risk in Emerging Markets:**
   - INR depreciation ~5% annually typical
   - BRL, ZAR more volatile (10-15% moves common)
   - Adjust valuations 10-15% for FX risk or require currency hedge

4. **Verification Discipline:**
   - All multiples should be 2-3 year historical range analysis
   - Peer comparison: Identify truly comparable companies
   - Analyst consensus: Check if your valuation deviates >30% from consensus
   - Sensitivities: Model if key assumption changes 1% what happens?
